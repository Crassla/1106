Arzan's Ethical Reflection:


The usage of machine learning (ML) has been increasing overtime especially in recent years. This is because it has become very relevant in today’s society due to a lot of new technology coming out that heavily depends on machine learning to take care of data accuracy to reduce labour hours and therefore labour costs such as modern electronic health records, robotic projects (such as drones and self-driving cars), and digital devices (such as surveillance cameras and wearable/portable devices).

However, despite all of these advantages, machine learning has a lot of disadvantages. One major disadvantage of machine learning is the rise in ethical concerns for machine learning. One relevant ethical concern in our current society is the concern for the use of machine learning in the military.

Considering the facts that the military uses special drones that use machine learning to navigate and detect specific people, these drones are loaded with ammo and are sent into battlefields instead of foot soldiers (this is so that soldiers do not need to endanger their lives when a machine can just do it for them), and the fact that they could possibly be hacked into and innocent people in other countries could die raises a very big ethical concern.

Another relevant ethical concern in our current society is the fact that machine learning is being used in the medical field to deliver accurate and reliable data for medical professionals so they can treat their patients with the utmost care. But this raises the ethical concern of what would happen if the machine learning process failed. What if the medical professionals could not understand the reasoning behind the machine learning’s data. This would put the patients at risk and could even lead to death if the prescriptions are wrong. This could have a very negative effect on the patients’ families.

Another question that arise from this situation is who would take the blame, the medical professional or the machine. These are all very important factors to consider before using machine learning. Machine learning has a lot of advantages but one little slip up could have deadly consequences.
